Diabetes Insipidus

Diabetes Insipidus (DI) is the most common complication of pituitary surgery. Although there is no consensus on its definition, polyuria is the most overt symptom of DI and can also reflect several physiological adaptive mechanisms in the postoperative phase.

Symptoms of DI:

  • Polyuria:  Urine production > 300 mL/hr for 2 consecutive hours
  • Hypotonic Urine:   Urine specific gravity (USG) < 1.005
  • Serum Osmolality:   Serum osmolality > 300 mosmol/kg
  • Serum Sodium:   Serum sodium > 145 mmol/L
  • Excessive Thirst


To improve reliability and comparability, the following definition for DI was used: polyuria (urine production > 300 ml/hour for 3 hours) accompanied by a urine specific gravity (USG) < 1.005 and at least one of the following symptoms: excessive thirst, serum osmolality > 300 mosmol/kg, or serum sodium > 145 mmol/L.
